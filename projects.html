This project explores the role of collaborative software platforms in modern science through an analysis of GitHub's application in AI-driven drug discovery. It aims to understand the effects of open-source code sharing on research transparency and reproducibility, providing insights into best practices for computational research. The study uses a review of platform capabilities and community practices and contributes to broader discussions on accelerating scientific progress through open science and collaborative tool development.

Automating Drug-Drug Interaction Prediction

Evaluates the impact of [model/tool/method] on [region/topic]. Looks into projections and long-term outcomes under different scenarios...

This project explores the prediction of adverse drug-drug interactions through a comparative analysis of the AutoGluon Automated Machine Learning (AutoML) framework. It aims to understand the effects of different molecular feature representations on predictive performance, providing insights into automating and improving pharmacovigilance. The study uses molecular data from the DrugBank database and the RDKit cheminformatics toolkit and contributes to broader discussions on balancing model performance with interpretability in computational drug safety.

Large Language Models in Drug Delivery

This study investigates the socio-economic impacts of infrastructure development on local communities...

This project explores the potential of Large Language Models (LLMs) to overcome formulation challenges through a comprehensive review of the current landscape. It aims to understand the effects of applying general-purpose AI models to specialized pharmaceutical problems, providing insights into accelerating the design of drug delivery systems. The study uses an evaluation of existing LLM-powered tools like ChemCrow and BioBERT and contributes to broader discussions on integrating artificial intelligence into experimental workflows and personalized medicine.
